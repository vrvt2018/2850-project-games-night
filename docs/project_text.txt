Assignment type and

In this assignment you will work on a given project with a group of your 
peers to produce a web application. You will be responsible for 
developing the requirements, designing, and evaluating your user 
interface and user experience, and incorporating user feedback to 
iteratively develop a system. 
Rationale 
As a software engineer, you need to be able to work across all aspects 
of the project, not only writing the code but also understanding your 
users and their needs, producing suitable documentation, and 
developing applications which meet those needs.

1 Assignment guidance 
You will produce a web application based on one of several different system specifications. 
The specification will provide a basic high-level overview of the system. You will use this to help plan 
a needs-finding discussion with a product owner, which you will then use to develop a 
comprehensive set of user stories which will be prioritised, and developed to produce a working 
webapp. 
You will work as a self-organising team, producing and iteratively improving a system based on your 
initial needs-finding and from peer and product owner feedback. You will use different testing 
methods including unit testing, UX testing and end-to-end testing throughout development and use 
this to shape your development. 
As a group, you will select and use appropriate tools to manage your work, develop your code and 
handle version control. You will also develop some minimal documentation, both of your system and 
of your design and testing. 
You and your team will also be responsible for organising weekly team meetings and at least two 
retrospectives throughout your project ? minutes and actions will be taken for these meetings.

You must also test your code, and your tests will form a part of your assessed work.

You should keep older versions of these files as you adapt and modify them, with notes about any

Individual Reflection 
Each week there will be a portfolio task of completing a weekly development diary ? this is a short 
set of reflective questions for you to answer on Gradescope and will help to evidence your work on 
the project. 
The project runs over 7 weeks (week 4 ? week 11) ? you must complete a minimum of 5 
weekly development diaries in this time in order to pass portfolio requirements. 
To ensure that diaries are an accurate reflection of the week, there is a strict time window to 
complete the previous week?s diary. 
At the end of the project, you will complete a short (~2000 word) reflection on your personal growth 
throughout the project. A template will be provided for this reflection, and you will be able to use 
your weekly diaries to remind yourself of previous weeks.

At the end of the project, you will do a final presentation and demonstration of your work. You will be

You will demonstrate your system- this should be a practiced professional demonstration which is 
based on personas and job stories and demonstrates the full functionality of the system. This should 
take around 15 minutes. 
This demonstration will be to 2 members of staff.

Team Allocation 
You will work in a team of 4, and teams are assigned using a survey tool which will match you 
based on several criteria: 
- 
Students who want to do the same projects (priority 1) 
- 
Students who have a similar timetable (priority 2) 
- 
Students with different working styles (priority 3) 
This is designed to be the fairest way of creating groups, ensuring that you can work on the project 
you want to, that you have time to do so, and that you are working with a range of different styles in 
your group. 
You cannot change teams, and you will need to work with people who you may not know ? this is 
part of working life, and it?s a useful skill to learn how to get along with people who may have 
different interests, personalities and styles than yourself. If you struggle with groupwork and find 
yourself needing additional support, please reach out. 
In cases where there are serious disagreements, clashes, or unacceptable behaviour in a group, 
please email the module leader ASAP. 
 
 
 
3 General guidance and study support 
Team Working, Communication and Expectations 
You will be working in a team, meaning that you need to ensure you are communicating with your 
group and that everyone knows what everyone else is doing. You should have regular (weekly) 
meetings and should have some more frequent informal communication about your work. 
Meetings are a chance to discuss any ongoing issues, make decisions about design or changes, 
and make sure everyone is up to speed with where the system is. 
You will have a retrospective after each sprint which will be a way of changing working practices, 
resolving any issues or conflicts within the group, and incrementally improving how you work as a 
team. 
If you have serious conflicts within the group, or concerns about someone?s work, please raise them 
in your check-ins or by messaging module staff. Where the concerns are minor (e.g. someone?s 
code is not as high standard as expected) then please discuss this in group and handle this in a 
professional way such as pair-programming with the team member and supporting them to improve.  
If someone does not engage with the work, does not communicate with the team, etc. please raise 
this as early as possible to module staff. If that person does later engage with the group, please try 
and include them- make sure they are assigned some work to do and try and integrate them into the 
group. 
Individual Working 
At times, you will work alone on parts of the system or on code. You should ensure you are doing 
this to the standards set by your group, following expected conventions, and not making large 
changes to other peoples? code without discussion. 
You should track what you are working on using your project board to ensure everyone knows what 
everyone else is doing. 
Use of Git 
You will be using Git for version control throughout the module. You should, as a team, decide on a 
strategy for how you will work ? for example, whether you will use feature branches or developer 
branches, and how pull requests on main will be handled. 
You should not program directly on the main branch ? your main branch is for finished, tested, 
working code only. Code should be developed on branches and pull requests used to merge into 
Main only when tests have been passed, and code is confirmed to be working correctly.  
 
 
 
Testing 
You are expected to thoroughly test your code, both to ensure it does what you expect it to do, and 
to ensure that you have considered a range of potential security risks. You should try and make sure 
that there is no ?unknown? behaviour ? if we consider the example of the Wordle portfolio task, this 
involves asking yourself: 
- 
How does my program react if the words.txt file is empty? 
- 
How does my program react if the words.txt file doesn?t exist? 
- 
How does my program react to empty user inputs? 
- 
What happens if a word contains 4 letters, or numbers/special characters? 
Consider what may create exceptions in your program, how this should be handled gracefully, and 
what you can do to prevent your user receiving error messages. Remember ? you?re writing these 
programs for a customer. 
You should also plan this with your team ? think about how your classes and functions interact, and 
where the best place to put validation will be and therefore where the tests should be.  
It is generally recommended that the person who writes the code should not be the person who 
writes the tests. We also recommend trying test-driven development (writing the tests first). 
You may do some manual testing, for example testing full user journeys ? in these cases, consider 
how you document these, when they should be done, and how the feedback is given to the 
developers.

7 Academic misconduct and plagiarism 
Plagiarism and Academic Integrity 
Leeds students are part of an academic community that shares ideas and develops new ones.    
You need to learn how to work with others, how to interpret and present other people's ideas, and 
how to produce your own independent academic work. It is essential that you can distinguish 
between other people's work and your own and correctly acknowledge other people's work.   
All students new to the University are expected to complete an online?Academic Integrity tutorial and 
test, and all Leeds students should ensure that they are aware of the principles of Academic 
integrity.?  
When you submit work for assessment it is expected that it will meet the University?s academic 
integrity standards.?  
If you do not understand what these standards are, or how they apply to your work, then please ask 
the module teaching staff for further guidance.  
By submitting this assignment, you are confirming that the work is a true expression of your 
own work and ideas and that you have given credit to others where their work has 
contributed to yours.
